# 4.1.0 Operational Definitions of Love 2.0 and Hate 2.0

To ensure analytical clarity and technical applicability, this study adopts **operational definitions** of _Love 2.0_ and _Hate 2.0_ that are grounded in observable decision structures and behavioral outcomes rather than subjective intentions. These definitions are designed to support both normative analysis and implementation within AI-governed systems.

## 4.1.0.1 Love 2.0: Operational Definition

In this framework, _Love 2.0_ is defined as a **governance-oriented ethical orientation** characterized by the following necessary and jointly sufficient conditions:

### (1) Equality of Ethical Application

A decision, policy, or system output satisfies _Love 2.0_ if it applies **the same ethical standards to all affected subjects**, without differentiation based on identity, affiliation, power, or status.

Operationally, any rule or decision logic that yields systematically different treatment for similarly situated individuals fails to meet this criterion.

### (2) Non-Sacrificial Sustainability

_Love 2.0_ excludes outcomes whose stability or effectiveness depends on the **systematic sacrifice, suppression, or instrumentalization of particular individuals or groups**.

Operational assessment focuses on whether an outcome requires enduring asymmetrical burdens in order to persist.

### (3) Non-Adversarial Cognitive Framing

Decisions aligned with _Love 2.0_ do not rely on **enemy construction, binary moralization, or us-versus-them narratives** as explanatory or justificatory devices.

Operationally, any decision logic that requires the identification of an adversarial out-group in order to maintain coherence or legitimacy is classified as non-aligned.

### (4) Civilizational Behavioral Expression

_Love 2.0_ necessarily manifests in **observable civilized behaviors**, including respect, recognition, non-coercive interaction, and the avoidance of humiliation, violence, or domination.

Internal benevolent intent without stable behavioral expression is considered non-verifiable and thus insufficient for governance purposes.

### (5) Public Generalizability

Finally, _Love 2.0_ requires that decision principles be **publicly generalizable**, such that their widespread institutional adoption would predictably enhance trust, cooperation, and long-term social stability rather than fear or compliance.

This criterion distinguishes ethical orientations suitable for public governance from those effective only in localized or exceptional contexts.

---

## 4.1.0.2 Hate 2.0: Operational Definition

_Hate 2.0_ is defined as an alternative governance orientation that is operationally identified by the presence of **any** of the following characteristics:

### (1) Differential Ethical Treatment

Any decision framework that assigns different ethical standards or entitlements to subjects based on identity, affiliation, or power is operationally classified as _Hate 2.0_.

### (2) Justification of Asymmetrical Harm

Outcomes that justify persistent harm, deprivation, or exposure to risk for some subjects in order to stabilize or benefit others are categorized under _Hate 2.0_.

### (3) Adversarial World Modeling

Decision systems that simplify complex social realities into adversarial binaries (e.g., friend/enemy, pure/impure) are operationally aligned with _Hate 2.0_.

### (4) Barbaric Behavioral Outcomes

Any outcome that results in violence, dehumanization, coercion, or institutionalized humiliation is considered a _Hate 2.0_ manifestation, regardless of stated intent.


## 4.1.0 爱 2.0 与恨 2.0 的操作性定义

为确保分析的清晰性与技术上的可适用性，本文采用对**爱 2.0（Love 2.0）**与**恨 2.0（Hate 2.0）**的操作性定义。这些定义并不依赖于主观意图的推断，而是基于**可观察的决策结构与行为结果**，以同时支持规范性分析与在 AI 治理系统中的实际应用。

### 4.1.0.1 爱 2.0：操作性定义

在本研究框架中，**爱 2.0** 被界定为一种**以治理为导向的伦理取向**，其成立需同时满足以下**必要且充分的条件**：

#### （1）伦理适用的平等性

当某一决策、政策或系统输出对所有受影响主体适用**同一伦理标准**，且不因身份、隶属关系、权力或社会地位而产生差异化对待时，该决策或输出被视为符合爱 2.0。

在操作层面，任何导致处于相似情境的个体受到系统性差别对待的规则或决策逻辑，均不满足该条件。

#### （2）非牺牲型可持续性

爱 2.0 排除那些其稳定性或有效性依赖于对特定个人或群体进行系统性牺牲、压制或工具化的结果。

操作性评估重点在于：某一结果是否需要个体或群体长期承担不对称负担，方能维持其运行。

#### （3）非对抗性的认知框架

与爱 2.0 对齐的决策不依赖于“敌人建构”、二元道德化或“我们—他们”式叙事作为解释或正当化基础。

在操作上，任何必须通过界定对立他者或敌对群体才能维持其内在一致性或合法性的决策逻辑，均被视为不符合爱 2.0。

#### （4）文明性的行为外化

爱 2.0 必然体现为**可观察的文明行为**，包括尊重、承认、非强制性交互，以及对羞辱、暴力与支配行为的避免。

仅停留于内部善意意图、但缺乏稳定行为表达的情形，在治理层面被视为不可验证，因此不足以构成爱 2.0。

#### （5）公共可推广性

最后，爱 2.0 要求其决策原则具备**公共可推广性**，即当其被广泛制度化与长期实施时，能够可预期地增强社会信任、协作与整体稳定性，而非制造恐惧或被动服从。

该标准用于区分适用于公共治理的伦理取向，与仅在局部或例外情境中奏效的策略性取向。

---

### 4.1.0.2 恨 2.0：操作性定义

**恨 2.0** 被界定为一种与爱 2.0 相对的治理取向，其操作性识别基于以下任一特征的出现：

#### （1）伦理适用的差异化

任何基于身份、隶属关系或权力差异，对主体适用不同伦理标准或权利配置的决策框架，均在操作上被归类为恨 2.0。

#### （2）不对称伤害的正当化

凡是通过正当化对部分主体的持续性伤害、剥夺或风险暴露，以实现他者稳定或利益的结果，均属于恨 2.0。

#### （3）对抗性的世界建模

将复杂社会现实简化为对立二分结构（如朋友／敌人、纯洁／污秽）的决策系统，在操作上被视为与恨 2.0 对齐。

#### （4）野蛮性行为结果

任何导致暴力、去人化、强制或制度性羞辱的结果，无论其主观意图如何，均被视为恨 2.0 的具体表现。
